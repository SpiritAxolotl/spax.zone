Originally written December 31st, 2024
Hey! Spax here.
I did a LOT this year.
Good and bad, thoughtful and stupid, cool and boring, all that stuff.
Last year I wanted to do a retrospective for 2023 on cohost, but I never ended up doing it, so I made a promise to myself to do it this year.
(But not on cohost because it's dead in a couple hours. RIP cohost)
So, no better place to start than an immediate major blunder!
In late December (of 2023), an irl friend of mine wanted css help for their incremental browser game, so I volunteered and made a better stylesheet for it.
I ended up getting carried away with modifying it and rewrote basically all of the javascript. My friend did not like this and refused to merge the changes I made, and this was the start of a semi-long feud between me and her.
I kept maintaining my fork of her repository (even after her original repository went private). This was until around the middle of March when I finally realized that it's a better idea to maintain a good relationship with her rather than be elitist about code I wrote but still effectively stole from her.
I archived the repository and made a formal apology to her, and our relationship has basically fully recovered since. I still regret basically everything I did, save for one thing:
Over the course of the 3 months this went on, I had learned a fuck-ton of javascript, html, css, git, bash, game making/maintaining, et cetera. I learned a lot more than I had in previous years with the web dev in particular, so I figured I should work on my site again!
Speaking of which, let's jump back to February.
After getting a lot of opinions, I decided to buy spax.zone and move everything from spiritaxolotl.github.io to it.
The filepaths still weren't pretty, but it was nicer to, first: have a site that was actually owned by me, and second: have a shorter domain name.
Regardless, it was nice to have! I even changed my Bluesky to the domain.
The other domains I was considering getting were spax.dev, spax.space, spax.blue, spax.ooo, and more recently spax.fish. Maybe in the future I'll get them.
spax.zone felt nice though. Huge thanks to oatmealine for fully convincing me to get a .zone domain and be top-level domain neighbors!
Oh yea, yugoslavia.
If you know me well, you know about one of my favorite websites Ever: yugoslavia.best.
It's worth noting that I was pretty good friends with the owner of the site, mayflower. yugoslavia.best had a discord server that I was a part of and was pretty active in.
When that whole browser game feud happened, mayflower and the other admins caught wind of the situation and banned me from the server after I refused to stop. It was 100% deserved.
But now I can say that I was banned from yugoslavia!, which I think is very funny.
I suppose I should talk about less about negative things. Since we're still in February/March, I'll rapid-fire a few notable things that don't warrant an entire rant about:
I started listening to Aphex Twin! Really good artist with weird experimental music. I don't really know how to explain it, but it is definitely Autism music, and not everyone will vibe with it.
I really like in a room7 F760 and Acrid Avid Jam Shred in particular, but there's so many other ones that are so good. Remind me later to make a playlist of my favorite Aphex Twin songs.

Oh, this is a January thing, but I played Undertale Yellow.
Genuinely such a good Undertale fangame. Extremely faithful to the original without too many flaws. Great music, great art, great mechanics, and great extension of the lore. I highly recommend playing it if you've played Undertale.
Okay, let's move into April. I wrote a patch to NotITG that fixes one of the modifiers to work on MacOS and Linux. I turned 18 (finally) and travelled to London. Met up with the lead devs of Project Outfox and drank beer with them. I also played Rain World for the first time but not for very long.
Now let's hop back to late December 2023 again. I had failed not one, not two, but FOUR classes in my second quarter in senior year of high school, THREE of which were required classes.
Jumping back to late April, with basically one month before graduation, I REALLY NEEDED TO GET MY SHIT TOGETHER.
I went cold-turkey off of Discord while I took two of the three (required) failed classes online, and retaking the other one with my very willing teacher (who I'm so thankful to).
As an accidental replacement for Discord, I ended up using cohost a lot more than before, becoming extremely active and learning even more about html and css.
A lot of my time I should have spent locking in on my schoolwork, I spent on cohost instead, but it actually kept me sane through all of it. The schoolwork sucked ass.
I ended up writing a userscript to help automate parts of my online classes. Thank god I did that or else I would have actually had to learn the material!
I should do a dedicated blogpost about the entire month of May. So much happened during it regarding my schoolwork and cohost.
Something too good to skip though is animal gender bodypart: a silly website I made based on a cohost post I saw that poked fun at the tumblr usernames leaking into cohost, consisting of an animal, some sort of gendered term, and a common body part. I got a friend to style it nicely and now it's a fun thing to go to every so often.
Anyways, I figured everything out and was able to pass all my classes in time for graduation, so now I can finally say I've graduated high school! College is next up.
So now, it's June. I've graduated, and I decided to buy a dropout.tv subscription so I can watch some of their really good shows like Game Changer and Very Important People.

Unfortunately, my grandfather died right after I got it. We travelled to his state to attend his funeral, and then Cloudstrike happened and we were stuck in there for an extra few days haha. Very interesting experience.
Around this time, I met someone on cohost who got me out of my shell a bit with nsfw. I ended up breaking up with them a few months later due to our very different personalities, but it was a really nice couple of months when we were together.
It was ALSO around this time that I finally convinced my irl friend and primary artist to finally draw me a reference sheet for Nahua! I made a website to better communicate some extra details, as well as to have a permanent link to the most updated reference sheet.
